The coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) pandemic is an ongoing global pandemic caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). The virus was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, China. Hence, this visualisations aim to show trends of covid-19 cases by taking in several statistics which include: confirmed cases, deaths, recovered cases, active cases, seriousness of cases, and test cases.

Trends Over the Period

In Europe countries, it is known that various incentives were being offered to citizens to encourage vaccinations. "As of July 31, 88.6% of people above the age of 18 have taken the first dose and 72.1% of people in the same age group have taken the second dose in the country" (TheIndianEXPRESS, 2021). This might be the main factor where even though confirmed cases in European countries were high, the death cases were lower as compared to other continents which had same number of confirmed cases.

Australia/Oceania covid cases were lowest among all continents throughout the timeline where covid cases were under control. This was due to quick and decisive action from government based on incoming data, social capital builds trust amongst the public, and engagement with different communities to find out what they need (Forbes, 2021). On the other hand, South America is the only continent that had drop in covid cases in June 2021.
